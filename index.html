01

Introduction

About

I’m Cecilio. I’m curious, I like a steady pace, and I’m still learning how to show up well. Mission taught me that formation happens in ordinary fidelity: you show up, you tell the truth, and you stay kind when it costs you something.

Saint Paul’s Outreach formed how I listen and how I serve. At Boston College I’ve leaned into curriculum and instruction: how ideas get ordered, how students get treated with dignity, and how small habits turn into culture over time.

I want my teaching and leadership to feel human, careful, and built to last.

02

Foundations

Values

When things get loud, I come back to these three. They’re the backbone of how I want to coach and lead down the road.

  • Formation

    Character is trained, not wished into place. I want spaces where virtue can grow slowly, even when no one is clapping.

  • Discipline

    Freedom needs fences. Prayer, study, rest, small repeats: they keep leadership from turning into a performance.

  • Service

    If I have any authority, it should widen the circle for someone else. Classroom or neighborhood, same rule.
